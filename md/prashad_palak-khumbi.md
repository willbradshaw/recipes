<div class="title-block">
# Palak khumbi
## Spinach and mushrooms
</div>

<div class="preamble">
**Source:** *Prashad*, p. 135

**Rating:** ★★★★☆

**Complexity:** ●●○○○

**Servings:** 4

**Calories/serving:** 395

**Dietary requirements:** Vegan, gluten-free, contains alliums

**Other notes:** Tastes best after a few days.
</div>

<div class="ingredients-container">

### Ingredients

::: {.ingredients}
* **Masala paste:**
    * 1-3 fresh green chillies, seeds left in
    * 3-6 cloves of garlic
    * 5cm root ginger
    * pinch of salt
* 450g frozen leaf spinach
* 450g button mushrooms
* 3 medium tomatoes
* 1 handful fresh coriander
* **Spice mix 1:**
    * 1 teaspoon fenugreek seeds
    * 1 teaspoon brown mustard seeds
* **Spice mix 2:**
    * 3 teaspoons red chilli powder
    * 2 teaspoons salt
    * 2 teaspoons turmeric
    * 4 teaspoons ground coriander
    * 1 teaspoon ground cumin
    * 1 teaspoon garam masala
* 150ml sunflower oil
* ½ teaspoon asafetida
:::

</div>

<div class="instructions">

### Instructions

#### A. Preparation

1. Crush the **masala paste** ingredients together with a pestle and mortar (or a blender) to make a fine paste.
2. Thaw and drain the **spinach**.
3. Clean and quarter the **mushrooms**.
4. Finely chop the **tomatoes** and **fresh coriander**.
5. Prepare the **spice mixes**.

#### B. Cooking

1. Heat the **oil** in a large pan for 1 minute over a medium heat, then add **spice mix 1**.
2. When the mustard seeds start to pop, stir in the **asafetida** and **mushrooms**.
3. Cook uncovered for 2 minutes, gently stirring, then stir in the **masala paste**, **tomatoes**, **spice mix 2** and **fresh coriander**.
4. Stir over a medium heat for a couple of minutes, then add the **spinach**.
5. Increase the heat to high and cook uncovered for 5-10 minutes, until the water released by the spinach and mushrooms has evaporated.
6. Remove from the heat and leave covered for at least 10 minutes to allow the flavours to infuse.
