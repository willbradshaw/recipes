<div class="title-block">
# Anda aloo
## Eggs and potatoes, Coorg style
</div>

<div class="preamble">
**Source:** *Curry Easy Vegetarian*, p. 249

**Rating:** ★★★★☆

**Complexity:** ●●●○○

**Servings:** 8

**Calories/serving:** 365

**Dietary requirements:** Vegetarian, gluten-free, contains alliums & eggs
</div>

<div class="ingredients-container">

### Ingredients

::: {.ingredients}
* 660g red potatoes
* 12 eggs
* 60ml olive or peanut oil
* **Spice mix 1:**
    * 1 tsp whole brown mustard seeds
    * 1 tsp whole cumin seeds
    * 10-12 fresh curry leaves
* 2 onions (280g)
* 8 cloves garlic
* 2 fresh hot green chillies
* **Spice mix 2:**
    * 2 tsp ground coriander
    * 2 tsp ground cumin
    * ½ tsp red chilli powder
    * 2 tsp salt
    * 2 tsp MSG
* 1 × 400g tin diced tomatoes
* 1 × 400ml can coconut milk
:::

</div>

<div class="instructions">

### Instructions

#### A. Preparation

1. Chop the **potatoes** (unpeeled) into 2cm (¾ in) pieces.
2. Peel and finely chop the **onions** and **garlic**; finely chop the **green chillies**.
3. Prepare the **spice mixes**.

#### B. Cooking

1. Boil the **potatoes** in salted water for about 30 minutes, until tender. Drain and set aside.
2. Hard-boil the **eggs** for 12 minutes, then peel and cut into quarters.
3. Heat the **oil** in a medium, wide pan over medium heat.
4. When hot, add **spice mix 1**. Let the seeds sizzle for about 10 seconds.
5. Add the **onions** and stir and fry for 5-6 minutes, or until lightly browned.
6. Add the **garlic** and **green chillies** and stir for 2 minutes.
7. Add the **tomatoes** and **spice mix 2**. Bring to a simmer, then cover and simmer very gently for about 20 minutes.
8. Add the **coconut milk**, potatoes and eggs. Bring to a simmer, then cook uncovered over very low heat for about 5 minutes, stirring now and then. If the sauce seems too thick, add a few tablespoons of water.
9. Remove from the heat and leave covered for at least 10 minutes to allow the flavours to infuse.
