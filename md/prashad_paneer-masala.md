<div class="title-block">
# Paneer masala
## Paneer in tomato & onion sauce
</div>

<div class="preamble">
**Source:** *Prashad*, p. 142

**Rating:** ★★★★★

**Servings:** 4

**Dietary requirements:** Vegetarian, gluten-free, contains alliums

**Other notes:** Excellent paneer recipe. For best results, cook the onions until they're almost burnt.
</div>

<div class="ingredients-container">

### Ingredients

::: {.ingredients}
* 18oz (540g) paneer cheese
* **Masala paste:**
    * 2-4 fresh green chillies
    * 4-6 cloves of garlic
    * pinch of salt
* 6cm root ginger
* 1 medium onion
* 1 tin peeled or chopped tomatoes
* 2 handfuls fresh coriander/cilantro
* **Spice mix:**
    * 2 tsp salt
    * 2 tsp MSG
    * 2 tsp turmeric
    * 3 tsp ground coriander
    * 1 tsp ground cumin
* Sunflower oil
* 4 tbsp unsalted butter
* Up to 100ml hot water
* 1 tsp garam masala
:::

</div>

<div class="instructions">

### Instructions

#### A. Preparation

1. Chop the **paneer** into 2cm cubes, then toss them in 1½ tsp salt to prevent sticking.
2. Crush the **masala paste** together with a pestle and mortar.
3. Crush or finely chop the **ginger**.
4. Finely chop the **onion**, **tomatoes** and **fresh coriander**
5. Prepare the **spice mix**.

#### B. Cooking

1. Pour the **oil** into a large pan until about 1cm deep and heat over a high heat. (Alternatively, use a deep fat fryer.)
2. Gently lower the **paneer** cubes into the oil and fry until golden brown on all sides. Drain on kitchen towel until needed.
3. In a fresh pan, heat 75ml **oil** for 2 minutes over a medium heat.
4. Add the **masala paste** and fry for 20 seconds, stirring constantly.
5. Increase the heat to high, add the **onion** and fry until dark brown. To do this, let cook covered for 2 minutes, then stir and recover; repeat until dark brown.
6. Reduce the heat to low and add the **tomatoes**, **ginger**, **spice mix** and **fresh coriander**.
7. Return the heat to high and simmer uncovered for a minute, then add the **paneer** and **butter**, plus **hot water** as needed.
8. Cover and simmer over a low heat for 8 minutes, then stir in the **garam masala**.
9. Remove from the heat and leave covered for at least 10 minutes to allow the flavors to infuse.
