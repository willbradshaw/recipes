<div class="title-block">
# Dum aloo
## Bay-leaf infused new potatoes
</div>

<div class="preamble">
**Source:** *Prashad At Home*, p. 90

**Rating:** ★★★★☆

**Servings:** 4

**Dietary requirements:** Vegan, gluten-free, contains alliums

**Other notes:** Solid potato recipe.
</div>

<div class="ingredients-container">

### Ingredients

::: {.ingredients}
* **Masala paste:**
    * 4-6 cloves of garlic
    * 4cm root ginger
    * pinch of salt
* 600g baby new potatoes
* Sunflower oil
* 2cm cinnamon stick
* **Spice mix 1:**
    * 2 Indian bay leaves
    * 1 tsp cumin seeds
    * ¼ tsp asafetida
* 1 medium onion, diced
* **Spice mix 2:**
    * 1 tsp red chilli powder
    * 1 tsp turmeric
    * 2 tsp salt
    * 1 tsp garam masala
    * 2 tsp ground coriander
    * 1 tsp ground cumin
* 1 tin peeled tomatoes
* 1 handful fresh coriander
:::

</div>

<div class="instructions">

### Instructions

#### A. Preparation

1. Boil the **potatoes** in their skins for 35 minutes, soak in cold water, then peel and cut into ~3cm cubes.
2. Crush the **masala paste** together with a pestle and mortar.
3. Prepare the **spice mixes**.
4. Chop or blend the **tomatoes** to a coarse pulp.
5. Finely chop the **onion** and **fresh coriander**.

#### B. Cooking

1. Pour the **oil** into a large pan until about 1cm deep and heat over a high heat. (Alternatively, use a deep fat fryer.)
2. Fry the **potatoes** until crispy and golden brown, then drain on kitchen towel until needed.
3. In a fresh pan, heat 75ml **oil** for 2 minutes over a medium heat.
4. Add the **cinnamon stick** and swirl through the warm oil for about 1 minute.
5. Add **spice mix 1**, swirl through the oil briefly, then add the **onion**.
6. Increase the heat to high and cook uncovered for 5 minutes.
7. Add the **masala paste** and **spice mix 2**, stir through and cook for 30 seconds.
8. Add the **tomatoes**, bring to a simmer, cover and cook for 8-9 minutes.
9. Add the **potatoes** and **fresh coriander**, reduce the heat to medium and cook covered for 4 minutes.
10. Remove from the heat and leave covered for at least 10 minutes to allow the flavours to infuse.
