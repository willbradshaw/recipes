<div class="title-block">
# Paneer makhani
## Paneer in butter-tomato sauce
</div>

<div class="preamble">
**Source:** *Curry Easy Vegetarian*, p. 262

**Rating:** ★★★★☆

**Servings:** 4-6

**Calories/serving:** 520

**Dietary requirements:** Vegetarian, gluten-free, contains dairy

**Other notes:** Very quick and easy, great taste-to-effort ratio
</div>

<div class="ingredients-container">

### Ingredients

::: {.ingredients}
* **Sauce mix:**
    * 250ml tomato passata
    * 250ml double cream
    * 1 teaspoon garam masala
    * 2 teaspoons lemon juice
    * 1 teaspoon sugar
    * 2 teaspoons red chilli powder
    * 1 teaspoon ground cumin
    * 1 tablespoon dried fenugreek leaves (*kasuri methi*)
* 2 teaspoons fresh ginger
* 2 teaspoons salt
* 400g fresh Indian cheese (paneer)
* ½ teaspoon black pepper
* 3 tablespoons unsalted butter
* 1 teaspoon whole cumin seeds
* 2-3 tablespoons fresh coriander
:::

</div>

<div class="instructions">

### Instructions

#### A. Preparation

1. Crush or finely chop the **ginger**.
2. Finely chop the **fresh coriander**.
3. Combine the **sauce mix** ingredients with the **ginger**, **fresh coriander** and 1 teaspoon of the **salt** in a large bowl. Stir thoroughly and set aside.
4. Chop the **paneer** into 2cm cubes, then toss them in a large bowl with 1 teaspoon of the **salt** and the **black pepper**.

#### B. Cooking

1. Melt the **butter** in a large pan over a medium heat, then add the **cumin seeds** and cook for 30 seconds.
2. Add the **paneer** to the pan and fry until they are browned on at least two sides.
3. Pour the tomato sauce over the top and stir to mix.
4. Bring to a simmer, then heat very gently for 4-5 minutes, stirring gently.
5. Remove from the heat and leave covered for at least 10 minutes to allow the flavours to infuse.
