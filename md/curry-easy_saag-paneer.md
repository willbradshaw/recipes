<div class="title-block">
# Saag paneer
## Spinach with paneer
</div>

<div class="preamble">
**Source:** *Curry Easy Vegetarian*, p. 265

**Rating:** ★★☆☆☆

**Complexity:** ●●○○○

**Servings:** 6

**Calories/serving:** 403

**Dietary requirements:** Vegetarian, gluten-free, contains alliums & dairy
</div>

<div class="ingredients-container">

### Ingredients

::: {.ingredients}
* 510g (18 oz) paneer
* 2 tsp salt
* 285g (10 oz) frozen spinach
* 1 × 400g (14 oz) tin chopped tomatoes
* 3 generous handfuls fresh coriander leaves
* 2-3 fresh hot green chillies
* 45 ml dried fenugreek leaves (*kasuri methi*)
* 2 small onions
* **Masala paste:**
    * 5 tsp fresh ginger
    * 3 cloves garlic
* 60 ml olive or peanut oil
* **Spice mix:**
    * 1 tsp chilli powder
    * 3 tsp ground coriander
    * 2 tsp ground cumin
    * 1 tsp ground turmeric
    * 2 tsp MSG
:::

</div>

<div class="instructions">

### Instructions

#### A. Preparation

1. Cut the **paneer** into 2 cm (¾ inch) cubes. Toss with the **salt** in a large bowl and set aside.
2. Thaw the **spinach**.
3. Peel and finely chop the **onions**.
4. Peel and crush the **ginger** and **garlic** together into a **masala paste**.
5. Roughly chop the **green chillies**.
6. Put the **tomatoes**, thawed spinach, **fresh coriander**, green chillies and **kasuri methi** into a blender. Blend until smooth and set aside.
7. Prepare the **spice mix**.

#### B. Cooking

1. Heat the **oil** in a large non-stick frying pan over medium heat.
2. When hot, add the **onion** and fry for 7-8 minutes, until soft and golden.
3. Add the **masala paste** and stir-fry for another 2-3 minutes, until the onion picks up a little colour.
4. Stir in the **spice mix**.
5. Pour in the blended mixture, then add the **paneer** along with any salt left in the bowl.
6. Stir and bring to a simmer, then cook over low heat for about 10 minutes, stirring gently now and then.
7. Remove from the heat and leave covered for at least 10 minutes to allow the flavours to infuse.
