<div class="title-block">
# Sem aloo
## Green beans with potatoes
</div>

<div class="preamble">
**Source:** *Curry Easy Vegetarian*, p. 65

**Rating:** ★★☆☆☆

**Complexity:** ●●○○○

**Servings:** 6

**Calories/serving:** 225

**Dietary requirements:** Vegan, gluten-free, no major allergens

**Other notes:** Goes well with pita bread
</div>

<div class="ingredients-container">

### Ingredients

::: {.ingredients}
* 4 tbsp olive or peanut oil
* **Spice mix 1:**
    * ½ tsp ground asafoetida
    * 1 tsp whole cumin seeds
    * 1 tsp nigella seeds (*kalonji*)
* 2 tbsp fresh ginger
* 2-4 fresh hot green chillies
* 90ml tomato purée
* 680g (24 oz) string beans
* 680g (24 oz) red potatoes
* **Spice mix 2:**
    * 1 tsp salt, or to taste
    * 1 tsp MSG
    * 1 tsp red chilli powder
* 1 tsp garam masala
:::

</div>

<div class="instructions">

### Instructions

#### A. Preparation

1. Peel and finely chop the **ginger**.
2. Cut the **green chillies** into fine rounds.
3. Top and tail the **green beans**.
4. Cut the **potatoes** (skin on) into ~2cm chunks.

#### B. Cooking

1. Boil the diced **potatoes** for 25-30 minutes, until tender. Drain.
2. Put the **oil** in a large frying pan and set over a medium-high heat. When hot, add **spice mix 1** and let sizzle for a few seconds.
3. Add the **ginger** and **green chillies**, lower the heat to medium-low and stir for a minute.
4. Add the **tomato purée** and stir until the paste turns thick and dark red.
5. Stir in 250ml water, plus the **green beans**, **potatoes** and **spice mix 2**. Mix well and bring to a simmer.
5. Cover and cook on a low heat until the beans are tender and the sauce has thickened, about 20 minutes.
6. If there is too much sauce, raise the heat and boil it off, stirring gently.
7. Stir in the **garam masala**.
8. Remove from the heat and leave covered for at least 10 minutes to allow the flavours to infuse.
