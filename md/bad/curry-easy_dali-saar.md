<div class="title-block">
# Dali saar
## Simple toor dal, Saraswat Brahmin style
</div>

<div class="preamble">

**Source:** *Curry Easy Vegetarian*, p. 146

**Rating:** ★★☆☆☆

**Complexity:** ●●○○○

**Servings:** 5

**Calories/serving:** 415

**Dietary requirements:** Vegan, gluten-free, contains alliums

</div>

<div class="ingredients-container">

### Ingredients

::: {.ingredients}
* 450g toor dal (*toor dal*)
* 1.5L water
* **Spice mix 1:**
    * 1 tsp ground turmeric
    * 1 tsp salt
    * 1 tsp MSG
* 60ml vegetable oil
* **Spice mix 2:**
    * ½ tsp ground asafoetida
    * 2 tsp brown mustard seeds
* 2-6 fresh hot green chillies
* 2 dried hot red chillies
* 6-8 garlic cloves
* 20-24 fresh curry leaves
:::

</div>

<div class="instructions">

### Instructions

#### A. Preparation

1. Prepare the **spice mixes**.
2. Wash the **dal** several times in warm water until the water runs clear, then cover with fresh warm water and soak for 30 minutes. Drain.
3. Slice the **green chillies** crossways into thin rings.
4. Peel and cut the **garlic** cloves in half lengthways.

#### B. Cooking

1. Place the drained **dal** in a heavy-based pan and add the **water**. Bring to the boil, skimming off any froth that rises to the surface.
2. Stir in **spice mix 1**. Partially cover and cook for 35-40 minutes, until the dal is tender.
3. Heat the **oil** in a very small pan over medium-high heat.
4. When hot, add **spice mix 2** and stir until the mustard seeds start to pop.
5. Add the **green chillies**, **dried red chillies** and **garlic**. Stir until the garlic pieces turn light brown on both sides.
6. Crush the **curry leaves** in your hand and add to the pan. Stir once.
7. Immediately tip the contents of the small pan over the dal. Cover to entrap the aromas.
8. Remove from the heat and leave covered for at least 10 minutes to allow the flavours to infuse.
