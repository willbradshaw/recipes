<div class="title-block">
# Banarasi aloo
## Potatoes, Banarasi style
</div>

<div class="preamble">

**Source:** *Curry Easy Vegetarian*, p. 100

**Rating:** ★★★★☆

**Complexity:** ●●○○○

**Servings:** 6

**Calories/serving:** 330

**Dietary requirements:** Vegan, gluten-free, no major allergens

</div>

<div class="ingredients-container">

### Ingredients

::: {.ingredients}
* 100ml vegetable oil
* **Spice mix 1:**
    * ½ tsp ground asafoetida
    * 2 tsp brown mustard seeds
    * 1 tsp cumin seeds
* 4 tsp fresh ginger
* 3-4 fresh hot green chillies
* **Spice mix 2:**
    * 3 tsp ground coriander
    * 1 tsp ground turmeric
    * 1 tsp red chilli powder
    * 3 tsp salt
    * 3 tsp MSG
* 1350g waxy potatoes
* 4 tsp ground amchur (*amchur*)
* 2 tsp garam masala
:::

</div>

<div class="instructions">

### Instructions

#### A. Preparation

1. Prepare the **spice mixes**.
2. Crush or finely chop the **ginger**.
3. Finely chop the **green chillies**.
4. Cut the **potatoes** (skin on) into ~2cm chunks.

#### B. Cooking

1. Boil the diced **potatoes** for 25-30 minutes, until tender. Drain.
2. Heat the **oil** in a large non-stick frying pan or wok over medium heat.
3. When hot, add **spice mix 1** and stir until the mustard seeds start to pop.
4. Add the **ginger** and **green chillies**. Stir for a few seconds.
5. Add **spice mix 2**. Stir once.
6. Add the boiled potatoes. Stir and fry for about a minute, until well coated.
7. Add the **amchur** and **garam masala**. Stir gently and cook for another minute.
8. Remove from the heat and leave covered for at least 10 minutes to allow the flavours to infuse.
