<div class="title-block">
# Flower Vatana Nu Shaak
## Cauliflower with Peas
</div>

<div class="preamble">
**Source:** *Curry Easy Vegetarian*, p. 77

**Rating:** ★★★★☆

**Servings:** 4

**Dietary requirements:** Vegan, gluten-free, no major allergens

**Other notes:** Tasty and easy
</div>

<div class="ingredients-container">

### Ingredients

::: {.ingredients}
* 1 large head of cauliflower
* 250g frozen peas
* **Spice mix:**
    * 1 tsp salt
    * 1 tsp MSG
    * 1 tsp ground turmeric
    * 1 tsp red chilli powder
    * 3 tsp ground coriander
* 1 tsp fresh ginger
* 1-2 fresh green chillies
* 1 × 400g tin of peeled or chopped tomatoes
* 1 handful fresh coriander / cilantro
* 3 tbsp olive or peanut oil
* generous pinch of ground asafoetida
* 1 tsp whole brown mustard seeds
:::

</div>

<div class="instructions">

### Instructions

#### A. Preparation

1. Chop the **cauliflower** into 5cm florets
2. Wash the **cauliflower** and put in a large bowl filled with water. Set aside for 10-30 minutes.
3. Defront the **peas** in warm water.
4. Prepare the **spice mix**.
5. Peel and finely chop the **ginger**.
6. Slice the **green chillies** crossways into thin rounds.
7. Finely chop the **tomatoes** and **fresh coriander**

#### B. Cooking

1. Heat the **oil** in a large, preferably non-stick frying pan over medium-high heat.
2. When hot, add the **asafoetida**. A second later, add the **mustard seeds**.
3. As soon as the seeds start to pop, add the **green chillies** and stir once.
4. Add the **tomato**, **spice mix**, **ginger** and **fresh coriander**. Stir and fry for a few minutes until the tomato thickens into a paste.
5. Drain and add the **cauliflower** and **peas**. Stir a few times, bring to a simmer, then lower the heat to medium-low.
6. Cover and cook gently, stirring now and then, for about 10 minutes, or until the cauliflower is tender.
7. Remove from the heat and leave covered for at least 10 minutes to allow the flavors to infuse.
