<div class="title-block">
# Fansi
## Green beans with tomato and carom
</div>

<div class="preamble">
**Source:** *Prashad At Home*, p. 50

**Rating:** ★★★☆☆

**Complexity:** ●●○○○

**Servings:** 4

**Calories/serving:** 310

**Dietary requirements:** Vegan, gluten-free, no major allergens

**Other notes:** I prefer the other green bean carom recipe from *Prashad*, but this one is allium-free.
</div>

<div class="ingredients-container">

### Ingredients

::: {.ingredients}
* **Masala paste**:
    - 2-4 fresh green chillies
    - 3cm root ginger
    - pinch of salt
* 450g fresh green beans
* 125ml sunflower oil
* 3 tsp carom seeds
* ¼ tsp asafetida
* **Spice mix**:
    - 2 tsp turmeric
    - 2 tsp salt
    - 2 tsp ground coriander
    - ½ tsp ground cumin
* 2 medium vine tomatoes, or 1 tin chopped or peeled
* 2 handfuls fresh coriander
:::

</div>

<div class="instructions">

### Instructions

#### A. Preparation

1. Crush the **masala paste** together with a pestle and mortar.
2. Top, tail & halve the **green beans**.
3. Prepare the **spice mix**.
4. Chop or blend the **tomatoes** to a coarse pulp.
5. Finely chop the **fresh coriander**.

#### B. Cooking

1. Heat the **oil** in a large thick-based pan for 2 minutes over a medium heat.
2. Add the **carom seeds** and fry for 30 seconds (no more), then add the **asafetida** and **green beans**.
3. Stir and turn the beans to make sure they are all covered in oil, then let them sizzle gently for a few seconds.
4. Add the **masala paste** and **spice mix** & stir through.
5. Cover and cook on a medium heat for 7 minutes, stirring every few minutes.
6. Stir in the **tomatoes** and **fresh coriander**.
7. Re-cover, reduce the heat to low and cook for a further 7 minutes.
8. Remove from the heat and leave covered for at least 10 minutes to allow the flavours to infuse.
