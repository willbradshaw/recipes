<div class="title-block">
# Renghan lilva
## Aubergine and broad bean curry
</div>

<div class="preamble">
**Source:** *Prashad*, p. 110

**Rating:** ★★★★☆

**Servings:** 4

**Dietary requirements:** Vegan, gluten-free, no major allergens

**Other notes:** Keeps well
</div>

<div class="ingredients-container">

### Ingredients

::: {.ingredients}
* 1 × 400g tin broad/fava beans
* **Masala paste:**
    * 2-4 fresh green chillies
    * 4 cloves of garlic
    * 3 tsp root ginger
    * pinch of salt
* **Spice mix 1:**
    * 1 teaspoon brown mustard seeds
    * 2 teaspoons carom seeds
* **Spice mix 2:**
    * ½ tsp asafetida
    * 2 tsp turmeric
    * 3 tsp ground coriander
    * 2 teaspoons salt
    * 2 teaspoons sugar
* 2 large aubergines
* 2 handfuls fresh coriander
* 2 medium tomatoes
* 100ml sunflower oil
:::

</div>

<div class="instructions">

### Instructions

#### A. Preparation

1. Drain and rinse the **beans**.
2. Crush the **masala paste** together with a pestle and mortar (or a blender), to make a fine paste.
3. Prepare the **spice mixes**.
4. Cut the **aubergines** into 2cm cubes.
5. Finely chop the **tomatoes** and **fresh coriander**.

#### B. Cooking

1. Heat the **oil** in a large pan for about 90 seconds over a medium heat.
2. Add **spice mix 1**, stir briefly, then reduce the heat to low.
3. Add **spice mix 2** and stir briefly, then stir in the **masala paste**, **beans**, **aubergines** and half the **fresh coriander**, plus water if needed to bring up the volume.
4. Cover the pan and bring the mixture to the boil, then reduce the heat to low and simmer for 5-6 minutes.
5. Stir in the **tomatoes** and the rest of the **fresh coriander**, then re-cover and cook for a further 5 minutes.
6. Remove from the heat and leave covered for at least 10 minutes to allow the flavors to infuse.
