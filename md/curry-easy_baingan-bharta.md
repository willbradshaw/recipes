<div class="title-block">
# Baingan bharta
## Simple, twice-cooked aubergine
</div>

<div class="preamble">
**Source:** *Curry Easy Vegetarian*, p. 56

**Rating:** ★★★☆☆

**Servings:** 4

**Dietary requirements:** Vegan, gluten-free, contains alliums
</div>

<div class="ingredients-container">

### Ingredients

::: {.ingredients}
* 3 medium aubergines
* 60 ml vegetable oil
* **Spice mix 1:**
    * 1 tsp whole cumin seeds
    * 1 tsp whole fennel seeds
    * 1 tsp nigella seeds (*kalonji*)
* **Spice mix 2:**
    * 2 tsp salt
    * 2 tsp MSG
* **Masala paste:**
    * 6 tsp ginger
    * 4 tsp garlic
    * 1-2 fresh green chillies
* 1 medium onion (200-250g)
* 2-3 medium tomatoes (300-400g)
* 1 handful fresh coriander
:::

</div>

<div class="instructions">

### Instructions

#### A. Preparation

1. Preheat an oven to 200ºC/390ºF.
2. Prick the **aubergines** all over with a fork, then place in a roasting tin.
3. Prepare the **spice mixes**.
4. Combine the **masala paste** ingredients together in a mortar and pestle (or a blender) into a smooth paste.
5. Peel and finely chop the **onion** and **tomatoes**.
6. Finely chop the **fresh coriander**.

#### B. Cooking

1. Place the **aubergine** in a roasting tin and bake in the oven for 1¼ hours, until tender.
2. Meanwhile, heat the **oil** in a large nonstick pan over a medium-high heat.
3. Add **spice mix 1** to the oil and let sizzle for a few seconds.
4. Add the **onion** and sauté for 5–6 minutes, until just starting to brown at the edges.
5. Add the **masala paste** and cook for a further 2 minutes.
6. Add the **tomatoes** and cook for 5–6 minutes, until softened.
7. When cooked, remove the aubergine from the oven, peel and finely chop.
8. Add the **aubergine** and **spice mix 2** to the pan, then stir and cook for 5 minutes.
9. Finally, stir in the **fresh coriander** and cook for a further 3 minutes.
