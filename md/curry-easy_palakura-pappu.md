<div class="title-block">
# Tuvar dal with spinach
## Palakura pappu
</div>

<div class="preamble">
**Source:** *Curry Easy Vegetarian*, p. 148

**Rating:** ★★★☆☆

**Complexity:** ●●●○○

**Servings:** 7

**Calories/serving:** 315

**Dietary requirements:** Vegan, gluten-free, contains alliums

**Other notes:** Don't overdo the tamarind
</div>

<div class="ingredients-container">

### Ingredients

::: {.ingredients}
* 400g tuvar dal
* 200g frozen spinach
* 2 medium onions
* 2-4 fresh hot green chillies
* **Spice mix 1:**
    * 1 tsp ground turmeric
    * 2 tsp salt
    * 2 tsp MSG
* **Spice mix 2:**
    * ½ tsp ground asafoetida
    * 1 ts whole brown mustard seeds
    * 4 dried red chillies
* 1 × 400g tin of peeled or chopped tomatoes
* 3 tsp tamarind concentrate
* 1 tsp red chilli powder
* 60 ml vegetable oil
* 6 cloves garlic
* 10-20 dried curry leaves
:::

</div>

<div class="instructions">

### Instructions

#### A. Preparation

1. Soak the **toovar dal** in a generous covering of water for about 1 hour, then drain.
2. Soak the **spinach** in water until thawed, then drain.
3. Finely chop the **onion**.
4. Slice the **green chillies** into thin rings.
5. Crush or chop the **garlic** into a fine paste.
6. Prepare the **spice mixes**.

#### B. Cooking

1. Put the **dal** in a heavy-based pan, add 1500ml water and bring to the boil over a high heat. Skim the froth off the surface.
2. Add the **spinach**, **tomato**, **onion**, **green chillies** and **spice mix 1** to the dal pan. Stir, then partially cover and cook gently for 40 minutes.
3. Add the **tamarind concentrate** and **chilli powder**, stir, then continue cooking partially covered for a further 15 minutes.
4. Put the **oil** in a small frying pan over a medium-high heat. When hot, add **spice mix 2** and stir for a few seconds.
5. Add the **garlic** to the frying pan and stir fry until lightly browned.
6. Crush the **curry leaves** lightly in your hand, then add to the frying pan.
7. Quickly tip the contents of the frying pan over the dal, then quickly cover to entrap the aromas.
8. Allow to sit for at least 15 minutes for the flavors to infuse.
