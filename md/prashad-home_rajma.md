# Rajma
## Kidney beans, classic Punjabi style
</div>

<div class="preamble">
**Source:** *Prashad At Home*, p. 60

**Rating:** ★★★★★

**Complexity:** ●●○○○

**Servings:** 4

**Calories/serving:** 430

**Dietary requirements:** Vegan, gluten-free, contains alliums

**Other notes:** One of my favourite recipes from this book.
</div>

<div class="ingredients-container">

### Ingredients

::: {.ingredients}
* **Masala paste**:
    - 2-4 fresh green chillies
    - 2-4 cloves of garlic
    - 4cm root ginger
    - pinch of salt
* 100ml sunflower oil
* 2 tsp cumin seeds
* ½ tsp asafetida
* 1 medium onion
* 1 tin peeled or chopped tomatoes
* **Spice mix:**
    * 1 tsp turmeric
    * 3 tsp ground coriander
    * 1 tsp ground cumin
    * 2 tsp salt
    * 1 tsp sugar
* 2 handfuls fresh coriander
* 3 × 400g tins kidney beans in brine,
thoroughly rinsed and drained
:::

</div>

<div class="instructions">

### Instructions

#### A. Preparation

1. Crush the **masala paste** together with a pestle and mortar.
2. Finely chop the **onion** and **fresh coriander**.
3. Chop or blend the **onion** and **tomatoes** to a smooth texture (separately).
4. Prepare the **spice mix**.
5. Finely chop the **fresh coriander**.
6. Thoroughly rinse and drain the **kidney beans**.

#### B. Cooking

1. Heat the **oil** in a large thick-based pan for 2 minutes over a medium heat.
2. Add the **cumin seeds** and swirl through the hot oil until they start to froth.
3. Add the **asafetida** and then the **onion**. Cover and cook until dark brown, checking and stirring occasionally.
4. Stir in the **tomatoes** and cook uncovered for 1 minute.
5. Add the **masala paste**, **spice mix** and **fresh coriander**, then cook uncovered for 1 minute.
6. Add the **hot water** and cook covered for 2 minutes.
7. Add the **kidney beans** and cook covered for 3 minutes.
8. Remove from the heat and leave covered for at least 10 minutes to allow the flavours to infuse.
